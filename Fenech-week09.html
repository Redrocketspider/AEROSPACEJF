Passenger SpaceJet

Recently, the space plane VF-56 hypersonic passenger plane has aced a series of tests for integrity and reusability. More tests are underway to ensure safety for public use by 2040. It is meant to carry hundreds of passengers from places like Los Angeles, California to Venice, Italy in under 3 hours of peaceful sub-orbital flight. It is a promising aspect that is getting closer to reality every day as technology advances and knowledge improves. Click here for more information.

Cruise SpaceJet

On March 20, 2021, AEROSPACEJF has successfully managed to complete a series of tests for the SpaceJet 1 on May 2020. It has since made multiple cruises for leaders of various nations, all over the world. The plane is now undergoing tests for use by civilians. It is expected to be available to the public by late 2040. Click here for more information.

Orbital Spaceplane

There has been a renewed interest in spaceplanes starting in 21st century when a private company started testing out planes designed with the capability of space flight. One such space plane is the PA-41, designed for commercial use and tested multiple times with success for hypersonic orbital flight. It is intended to be available to public use for passengers and cargo by 2030. Click here for more information.
